So, how do we calculate bezier curves?

So, I'll just give you the formula first. The formula for calculating a bezier curve is! *Drum roll*(1-t)*P0 + t(P1). Its less complicated looking than it seems huh(Just to let you know, I'm using the wonderful literary device known as foreshadowing). Let's break down this formula. First off, what's P0 and P1? They refer to coordinates. These coordinates define where our curve starts and ends. The "t" or T value refers to the percentage of the line. If say you want to get the coordinates of 50% along a line, you can use this formula to get the coordinate. Oh by the way, this formula won't yield a curve yet. All it does it give you a straight line.

Quadratic Bezier Curves

Here, we begin to actually get into making a curve. Ta da!. So, how is this curve even made? Essentially, this curve is the result of many interpolations. This gif over here will demonstrate.

So, what do I mean by many interpolations? What we do here is bassicaly first interpolating between our defined points or our control points, then interpolating the interpolation of those two points. This may seem complicated on paper but, when you look at it through the gif above, it makes more sense.

Cubic Bezier Curves

Cubic Bezier Curves are exactly like Quadratic bezier curves. Only with more interpolating and more complexity. This gif should help you visualise this. When we add more points onto a curve, we have to interpolate more and more. While we can reach an infinite number of points, it may not be the best solution to creating most shapes.

As you may have noticed, the more points we add on a bezier curve, the more complex shapes we can make. Strictly speaking, it'd be much more efficient if we use multiple Quadratic or Cubic bezier curves to construct our shape but its if we need to be very specific with a particular line, adding more points may be the way to go.

Bezier Curves in games

Bezier Curves are also found in video games! Quite a few developers use bezier curves as a means of path finding. Instead of using percentage of the line, they use time. Try it out yourself!

Game developers usually automate this process by performing some calculations on enemy and player position rather than defining these points manually.
